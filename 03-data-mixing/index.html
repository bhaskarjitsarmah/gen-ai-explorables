Read it as a concept, not a law. The paper formalizes this as the Critical Mixture Ratio — the most domain data you can add before general ability degrades sharply. The exact curve shifts with the base model, domain similarity, and tricks like general-data replay, scaling-law annealing, and domain self-constraint — which is how Baichuan4-Finance succeeds at just 20% domain data.

Why it's hard · three tensions the whole field wrestles with

Specialization ⇄ Generalization

The dial above. Balance the two through the Critical Mixture Ratio and replay — neither domain-only nor arbitrary mixing wins.

Quality · Diversity · Scale

The "impossible triangle." Aggressive quality filtering (Ask-LLM drops 90% of data) speeds convergence but can strip rare, valuable, diverse examples.

Static ⇄ Dynamic

Fix the ratio up front (RegMix, scaling laws) or adapt it during training (Online Data Mixing, TiKMiX) as the model's data preferences evolve.
